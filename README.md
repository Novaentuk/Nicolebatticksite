## Putting it online (GitHub Pages)

Your site will live on a **free** service from GitHub called **GitHub Pages**.
Don't worry if that's new — here's the whole thing, step by step. It takes about
10 minutes the first time.

**What you'll need:** a free account at **github.com** (sign up if you don't have
one).

1. **Sign in** at [github.com](https://github.com).
2. Click the **"+"** in the top-right corner, then **"New repository."**
   (A "repository" is just a folder that lives on GitHub.)
3. Give it a name — for example **`nicole-battick-site`** — and click
   **"Create repository."**
4. On the next page, click the link that says
   **"uploading an existing file."**
5. **Open this "Nova site" folder on your computer, select everything inside it,
   and drag it all into the upload box** in your browser.
   *(Important: drag the files that are* inside *the folder — not the folder
   itself. `index.html` needs to end up at the top level.)*
6. Scroll down and click **"Commit changes"** (that just means "save").
7. Now click the **"Settings"** tab (top of the page), then **"Pages"** in the
   left-hand menu.
8. Under **"Branch,"** choose **`main`**, leave the folder as **`/ (root)`**,
   and click **"Save."**
9. Wait about a minute, then refresh. GitHub shows you a link like
   **`https://your-username.github.io/nicole-battick-site/`** — **that's your
   live website.** 🎉

**To update the site later:** repeat steps 4–6 with the changed files. GitHub
replaces the old versions automatically.

> Want your own web address like **nicolebattick.com** instead of the long
> github.io link? That's possible — just ask me and I'll talk you through it.
